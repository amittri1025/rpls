Who we are

Founded in 2004, Res Publican Law Society (RPLS) originated from a vision in a Bihar village, shaping its commitment to social and economic justice. Launched in New Delhi, RPLS is led by distinguished jurists, including Hon'ble Mr. Justice D.P. Wadhwa. Beyond legal initiatives, RPLS champions literacy, conducts vital studies, and supports marginalized communities. Legal Awareness Camps, held in villages and city slums, epitomize the concept of 'Justice at door step,' fostering positive change and empowerment.

Res Publican Law Society (RPLS) is a vision which appeared in a remote Bihar village during the dissemination of research finding in a workshop on “Study of Impact of Globalization on Marginalized Women and her Infant”. Gradually the vision crystallized and it acquired the shape of the present Society and it undertook the onerous task of working towards the attainment of social and economic justice.

The Society was launched on January 26, 2004 at New Delhi by one of its Chief Patron Hon'ble Mr. Justice D.P. Wadhwa the then Chairman of TDSAT and former Judge, Supreme Court of India in the presence of Hon'ble Mr. Justice Brijesh Kumar Judge, Supreme Court of India and Hon'ble Mr. Justice M.B. Shah, the then President NCDRC and former Judge, Supreme Court of India. RPLS got itself formally registered on March 4, 2005. Besides holding Seminars, organizing Symposiums and conducting Workshops, the Society is instrumental in promoting literacy for poor children. Other activities of the Society includes “Study on Nutritional levels of Mother and her Child”, helping Senior Citizens and Widows in getting governmental pensions. Legal Awareness Camps are regularly held at the level of villages in and around Delhi and city slums to actualize the concept of 'Justice at door step'.

Our Mission

Res Publican Law Society (RPLS) is dedicated to fostering a just and inclusive society through legal advocacy and transformative initiatives. Our mission is to empower the marginalized by promoting literacy, conducting impactful studies on maternal and child nutrition, and supporting senior citizens and widows. We strive to bring legal awareness to communities through camps in villages and city slums, ensuring that justice is accessible to all, irrespective of socio-economic backgrounds. RPLS commits to continuous efforts in advancing social welfare, education, and economic equality, working towards a world where every individual's rights are protected and respected.

Our Vision

At Res Publican Law Society (RPLS), we envision a world where social and economic justice prevail as fundamental rights. Born from a workshop in a Bihar village, our vision crystallized on January 26, 2004, into a commitment to creating tangible change. We aspire to be a beacon of hope, empowering the marginalized through literacy programs, nutritional studies, and support for vulnerable groups. Our vision extends beyond legal advocacy, embracing the principle of 'Justice at door step' through awareness camps in villages and city slums. RPLS envisions a society where equal access to justice, education, and well-being is a shared reality for all.
